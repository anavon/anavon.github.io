LIFE OF PI


illustration, cover design


This project started out as a passion project combining one of my favorite books with my ongoing obsession with traditional henna art and Indian fabric block printing. Though I didn't end up using printmaking techniques for the final image, I started with a hand-drawn base for the illustration before adding pattern, color, and texture digitally. Originally intended as a book cover, this illustration found new life when Volume One Magazine in Eau Claire commissioned me to create a cover for their August 2015 issue.

>> Volume One Magazine has been a staple for culture and entertainment in the Chippewa Valley since 2002. Currently they print 14,000 copies every two weeks and distribute for free at over 300 locations. Check out their cool stuff at volumeone.org.

>> Since Life of Pi is full of symbolism, I hid multiple symbols in the pattern of this illustration that tell different parts of Pi's story, including his religions, family, and the animals that appear throughout the narrative.
